About Me

I'm Matt Brems!

Currently, I'm a data science instructor with General Assembly based in Washington, D.C. I'm afforded the incredible opportunity and responsibility to train data scientists across the United States.

My philosophy of data science and my role in it:

  • Our world is made better when decisions are informed by data.
  • Data science is made better by properly relying on rigorous statistical methods.
  • Rigorous statistical methods are often inaccessible without serious academic training in mathematics and statistics.
  • Our world does not reach its full potential if we limit data science and data-driven decision-making to only those with serious academic training.

To this end, I'm particularly passionate about bridging the gap between theoretical statistics and real-world insights. I want to contribute my voice to the chorus of individuals who work tirelessly to make data-driven decision-making accessible, sexy, and fun.

In a prior life, I did data science work for a political consulting firm named 0ptimus (as in 0ptimus Prime), earned my Master's degree in statistics from Ohio State, and studied math, economics and political science at Franklin College.

Beyond General Assembly, I spend most of my time buying and partially reading statistics textbooks, overcaffeinating on coffee, re-watching television series on Netflix, or ordering unhealthy delivery food with my boyfriend and our pug.
